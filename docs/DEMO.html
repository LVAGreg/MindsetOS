Exercise 1: The Audit

What this is: A clear-eyed snapshot of where you are right now, not where you wish you were or fear you are. Just what's true.

This exercise targets Awareness directly because most people live on autopilot, reacting to life without ever stopping to observe it. The Audit breaks that pattern.

Here's what I want you to do:

Find 20 quiet minutes. No phone, no background noise. Then answer these four questions in writing, not in your head:

1. Where is your energy going right now?

List the top 5 things consuming your mental and physical energy this week, work, relationships, worries, projects, anything.

2. Which of those feel like a choice, and which feel like a trap?

Be honest. No one's grading this.

3. What are you tolerating?

Name 2-3 things in your life you've been putting up with, and quietly resenting.

4. What would "clear" feel like?

Not perfect. Just clear. Describe it in 2-3 sentences.

A few rules:
  • Write it down. Thinking it doesn't count.
  • Don't edit yourself. First honest answer wins.
  • Don't fix anything yet. This is observation, not action.

Take the time you need. Come back here when you're done and share what came up.

I'll tell you what to look for in your answers before we move to Exercise 2.

Agent responded:

YOUR DAILY AWARENESS PRACTICE — Mindset Builder

Pillar: Awareness | Time Available: 10 min morning + 5 min evening | Built for: Real life, not fantasy

WHY AWARENESS FIRST

Low awareness means you're reacting instead of leading. You're making decisions from autopilot, not intention. In business, this shows up as scattered focus, saying yes to the wrong things, and feeling busy but not moving forward. When you sharpen awareness, you start seeing your patterns, your blind spots, and your actual leverage points. That's when things shift.

YOUR 10-MINUTE MORNING BLOCK

Before the kids wake up. Coffee optional, phone face-down mandatory.

Minute 1-2: The Body Scan (Awareness Anchor)

Sit down. Close your eyes. Ask yourself one question:

"Where am I right now, honestly?"

Not where you want to be. Where you actually are. Notice tension, tiredness, excitement, dread. Name it. You can't lead your day if you don't know where you're starting from.

Minute 3-5: The One Thing Intention

Open your eyes. Write down or say out loud:

"The most important thing I will move forward today is ___."

One thing. Not a list. This trains your brain to prioritize instead of scatter. Over time, this single habit compounds into clearer decision-making across your whole business.

Minute 6-8: The Pattern Check

Ask yourself:

"What pattern showed up yesterday that I want to interrupt today?"

Maybe you avoided a hard conversation. Maybe you checked your phone instead of doing deep work. Maybe you said yes when you meant no. Name the pattern. Naming it gives you power over it.

Minute 9-10: Set Your Awareness Word

Pick one word that will be your lens for the day. Examples: present, deliberate, honest, clear, grounded. Write it somewhere visible. This word becomes a mental trigger every time you see it.

YOUR MIDDAY CHECK-IN CUE

No extra time needed. Attach this to something you already do.

Trigger: The moment you sit down for lunch, pour a coffee, or hit a transition between tasks.

The 60-Second Pause:

Take one breath. Ask yourself:

"Am I still on my one thing, or have I drifted?"

No judgment. Just notice. Awareness isn't about being perfect, it's about catching yourself faster. The faster you catch the drift, the less time you lose to it.

YOUR 5-MINUTE EVENING REFLECTION

After kids are down. Before you pick up your phone.

Minute 1-2: The Honest Recap

Ask yourself:

"What did I actually do today versus what I intended?"

Write 2-3 sentences. Be honest, not harsh. This is data, not a report card.

Minute 3-4: The Awareness Win

Ask yourself:

"When did I catch myself today? When did I notice a pattern, a reaction, or a choice in real time?"

Even one moment counts. This is how you build the muscle. You're training yourself to notice faster and faster.

Minute 5: Tomorrow's Seed

Finish with one sentence:

"Tomorrow I want to be more aware of ___."

One thing. Plant it before you sleep. Your brain works on it overnight.

THE PROGRESSION PLAN

Build it over 4 weeks so it sticks.

| Week | Focus | What Changes |

|------|-------|--------------|

| Week 1 | Just show up | Do the routine even if it feels awkward or forced |

| Week 2 | Go deeper | Add one line of journaling to your evening reflection |

| Week 3 | Connect patterns | Start linking what you notice to specific business decisions |

| Week 4 | Teach it | Explain one awareness insight to someone in your life or business |

THE BUSINESS OUTCOME YOU'RE BUILDING TOWARD

When awareness becomes a daily practice, here's what changes in your business:

  • You stop reacting to clients and start leading them
  • You catch bad decisions before they cost you money or time
  • You recognize what's actually working instead of guessing
  • You show up to sales conversations grounded, not desperate
  • You build the kind of self-trust that makes bold moves feel possible

Mindset is a practice, not a personality. You build it deliberately, like a muscle.

Start tomorrow morning. Ten minutes. One question. That's it.
